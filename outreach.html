CMS OpenData Outreach at Bengaluru, India

(January 2023)

In the summer of 2022, my research at Fermilab made it possible to analyse publicly released CMS OpenData from 2015 using Jupyter Notebooks. This eliminated the needed for the ROOT environment that is normally used. Since Python and Jupyter Notebooks are tools that high school students are often more familiar and comfortable with, I decided to extend my work and use it as a means of helping students try their hand at particle physics analysis in a manner similar to the Quarknet Project.

Since this project is the first of its kind to use Python instead of ROOT, I created the curriculum and projects from scratch. Following a brief introduction to particle physics and the CMS Detector, students learnt how to analyse CMS OpenData from 2015. I delivered this content in person in January 2023. Students from year 11 in Bengaluru, India participated and successfully plotted the Z boson resonance peak for dimuon processes.
