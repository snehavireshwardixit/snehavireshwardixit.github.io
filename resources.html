Getting Research Experience

As an undergraduate, getting research experience can feel hard! It helps a lot if you can find a professor at your home university whose research you like. You can then request to work on a project with them.

That might not always work out, though. It's alright! Here are some links that can help you find a research opportunity for undergraduates. Note that a lot of them, especially REUs funded by the NSF, are limited to U.S. Citizens. If your are an international citizen (like me!) a lot of work goes into checking which REUs accept students of your nationality.

Some links that helped me start my search:
NSF Physics REUs(for undergrads)
Internships at Fermilab (for high schoolers and undergrads)
PathwaysToScience Database (for high schoolers and undergrads)
CERN Summer Student Programme(for undergrads)
